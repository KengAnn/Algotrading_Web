      Strategy Analyser


      Strategy Analyzer is a powerful tool where the traders will be able to view the performance metrics of a particular strategy. This is useful for users to know how their strategies fared objectively. The metrics that Strategy Analyzer provide includes:

      1. Sharpe Ratio

      Measure of risk-adjusted return. Sharpe ratio describes how much excess return one will receive for the volatility of holding riskier asset. Generally, the greater the value of the Sharpe ratio, the more attractive the risk-adjusted return.

      2. Sortino Ratio

      A variation of Sharpe Ratio that differentiates harmful volatility from total overall volatility. The Sortino ratio takes an asset or portfolio's return and subtracts the risk-free rate, and then divides that amount by the asset's downside deviation. This is is a useful way for investors, analysts, and portfolio managers to evaluate an investment's return for a given level of bad risk.

      3. Calmar Ratio

      A gauge of the performance of investment funds such as hedge funds and commodity trading advisors. Calmar ratio uses maximum drawdown as a measure of risk which is more understandable and preferred by investors and traders.

      4. Drawdown

      A drawdown is a peak-to-trough decline during a specific period for an investment, trading account, or fund. Drawdowns are important for measuring the historical risk of different investments, comparing fund performance, or monitoring personal trading performance.

      5. Volatility

      A statistical measure of the dispersion of returns for a given security or market index. In most cases, the higher the volatility, the riskier the security. In the securities markets, volatility is often associated with big swings in either direction. Volatile assets are often considered riskier than less volatile assets because the price is expected to be less predictable.
